You want a new phone. It costs ₱15,000. Your daily allowance is ₱500. The math doesn't work — and traditional budgeting says either "save up in a separate account" or "just don't buy it."

Neither answer feels great. Separate savings accounts add friction. And "just don't buy it" ignores the fact that sometimes you *should* buy things that improve your life.

That's why we built the Flex Bucket.

## What's a Flex Bucket?

A Flex Bucket is a savings goal that lives inside your daily budget. Instead of setting money aside in a separate account, you tell ledgr: "I want to save ₱15,000 over the next 30 days."

ledgr does the math: ₱15,000 ÷ 30 = ₱500/day set aside. Your daily allowance adjusts to account for it — so if you had ₱500/day before, it might drop to ₱350/day while the Flex Bucket is active.

You see both numbers: what you can spend today, and how much you've saved toward your goal.

## How It's Different From Traditional Savings

Traditional savings advice says: "Pay yourself first. Move 20% to a savings account. Don't touch it."

That works if you have margin. But when your daily allowance is already tight, moving money to a savings account feels like lying to yourself. The money is "saved" but your daily life gets squeezed to the point where you dip into savings anyway.

The Flex Bucket is different because it's **intentional spending, not restriction**:

- You pick a specific thing you're saving for
- You choose how long to save
- Your daily allowance reflects the real amount available to you
- When you hit your goal, the Flex Bucket clears and your allowance bounces back

It's honest. There's no illusion of having more or less than you do.

## Use Cases

The Flex Bucket works for anything that doesn't fit in a single day's allowance:

- **Gadgets:** New earbuds, phone upgrade, laptop repair
- **Gifts:** Birthday presents, Christmas shopping, wedding contributions
- **Travel:** Weekend trip to Baguio, flight home for the holidays
- **Emergency fund starter:** Save ₱10,000 over two months as your first buffer

You can run one Flex Bucket at a time, keeping things simple. Once it's funded, start another — or enjoy the higher daily allowance.

## The Psychology of Intentional Spending

There's a big difference between "I can't afford this" and "I'm choosing to save for this." The first feels like deprivation. The second feels like a plan.

When your daily allowance drops because of a Flex Bucket, it doesn't feel like losing money. It feels like making progress. You can see the bucket filling up. You know exactly when you'll reach your goal. And when you finally make that purchase, it comes with zero guilt — because you planned for it, day by day.

> Saving isn't about saying no. It's about choosing what to say yes to.
