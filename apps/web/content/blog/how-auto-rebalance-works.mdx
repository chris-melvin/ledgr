It's Friday night. Friends invited you out. You spent ₱800 on a dinner that was absolutely worth it — but your daily allowance was ₱450.

In most budgeting apps, this is where the red numbers appear. The warning banners. The feeling that you've already failed and it's only week two.

In ledgr, something different happens: your remaining days quietly absorb the difference, and you wake up Saturday with a new number. No lecture. No guilt.

That's auto-rebalance.

## How It Works

Auto-rebalance is simple math with a big psychological impact. Here's a five-day example:

**Starting point:** ₱2,250 left for 5 days = **₱450/day**

| Day | Allowance | Spent | Remaining Pool |
|-----|-----------|-------|----------------|
| Mon | ₱450 | ₱300 | ₱1,950 |
| Tue | ₱488 | ₱500 | ₱1,450 |
| Wed | ₱483 | ₱400 | ₱1,050 |
| Thu | ₱525 | ₱800 | ₱250 |
| Fri | ₱250 | ₱250 | ₱0 |

Notice what happens on Tuesday. You underspent on Monday (₱300 vs ₱450), so Tuesday's allowance went *up* to ₱488. Then Thursday was a big day — ₱800 — so Friday adjusted down to ₱250.

The system is constantly recalculating. Every day is a fresh start built on reality, not a fixed target you set three weeks ago.

## Why This Matters

The number one reason people abandon budgeting apps is **guilt**. You overspend once, see the red numbers, feel like the whole month is ruined, and stop opening the app.

Auto-rebalance breaks that cycle. It says: "You spent more today. Here's your new number for tomorrow. Let's keep going."

It's the difference between a coach who yells at you for missing a shot and one who adjusts the play and keeps you in the game.

> Progress over perfection. That's the whole philosophy.

## It's Not Permission to Overspend

Auto-rebalance doesn't pretend the money didn't get spent. If you blow ₱2,000 on a Tuesday, Wednesday's allowance will be noticeably smaller. You'll *feel* it. But you'll feel it as information, not punishment.

The math is honest. The difference is in the framing. And framing matters more than most financial advice admits.
