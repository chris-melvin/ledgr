Every savings article says the same thing: "Save 20% of your income." "Build a 6-month emergency fund." "Invest early and often."

Great advice — if you have money to spare. But when your daily allowance is ₱450 and life costs ₱430 of it, saving 20% feels like a fantasy.

So let's start smaller. Way smaller.

## The Challenge

Save ₱100 a day. That's it. Not ₱100 more than you're spending — ₱100 total. Find one small expense you can skip or reduce and bank the difference.

₱100/day × 30 days = **₱3,000/month**. ₱36,000/year.

That's a new phone. An emergency fund starter. A round-trip flight home for the holidays. From one hundred pesos a day.

## Where to Find ₱100

You don't need to overhaul your lifestyle. You need one small swap per day:

| Instead of... | Try... | Savings |
|---------------|--------|---------|
| Grab (₱180) | Jeep (₱13-15) | ~₱165 |
| Milk tea (₱120) | Water from home | ₱120 |
| Food delivery (₱250+) | Cook at home | ₱100-150 |
| Convenience store snacks (₱80) | Packed snacks | ₱60-80 |
| Afternoon coffee (₱100) | Office coffee | ₱100 |

You don't need to do all of these. You need one. Per day. Rotate through them so it doesn't feel like deprivation.

## Why ₱100 Works When ₱5,000 Doesn't

Setting a ₱5,000 monthly savings goal is intimidating. Where does it come from? What do you cut? The number is too big to act on daily, so it becomes a monthly guilt trip.

₱100 is a daily decision. "Can I save ₱100 today?" is a question you can answer right now. Yes or no. If yes, great. If no, try again tomorrow. No shame, no elaborate plan.

**Small + consistent > big + occasional.** Always.

## Track It to Believe It

The magic of the ₱100 challenge is watching it compound. Day one feels like nothing. Day seven, you've saved ₱700 — still modest. But by day 30, that ₱3,000 is real. It's in your account. You did that.

Use your ledgr daily allowance as a guide. If your allowance is ₱500 and you only spend ₱400, that ₱100 difference is working for you. You can see it in your remaining balance — proof that small decisions have real weight.

## The Upgrade Path

After a month of the ₱100 challenge, you'll notice something: it's easier than you thought. Some days you'll save ₱200 without trying. Some days ₱50. The average tends to drift upward because you've built the awareness muscle.

That's when you can graduate:

- **₱100/day for 3 months** → ₱9,000 emergency fund starter
- **₱150/day** → ₱4,500/month, ₱54,000/year
- **₱200/day** → ₱6,000/month, enough to start a small investment

But don't rush. The ₱100 version works. Start there.

> You don't need to save more. You need to save consistently.
