Sweldo day hits different. The notification comes in, you check your balance, and for one beautiful moment — you feel rich.

Then the bills come. Rent, electricity, water, internet, SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG. Maybe a credit card payment. Maybe padala to your parents.

By the time you've mentally subtracted everything, that "rich" feeling is gone and you're left wondering: *how much can I actually spend?*

Let's answer that question right now.

## Step 1: Enter Your Take-Home Pay

Open [ledgr](/signup) and enter your actual take-home amount — what hits your bank account after tax and deductions. Not your gross salary, not your "expected" income. The real number.

**Example:** ₱25,000 semi-monthly (₱50,000/month)

If you get paid twice a month, set up each sweldo separately. Your daily allowance recalculates each time.

## Step 2: List Your Fixed Bills

These are the non-negotiables — the expenses that are the same (or close to the same) every period. For a typical Filipino worker, that might look like:

| Bill | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Rent / housing | ₱6,000 |
| Electricity | ₱1,500 |
| Water | ₱300 |
| Internet | ₱1,500 |
| SSS / PhilHealth / Pag-IBIG | ₱1,200 |
| Phone load / plan | ₱500 |
| Padala (family support) | ₱3,000 |
| **Total fixed** | **₱14,000** |

Your numbers will be different. The point is to capture the recurring costs that leave your account no matter what.

## Step 3: See Your Daily Allowance

₱25,000 − ₱14,000 = ₱11,000 remaining

If your next sweldo is in 15 days: **₱11,000 ÷ 15 = ₱733/day**

That's your number. ₱733 for food, transport, entertainment, and everything else. Every day, recalculated based on what you actually spend.

## What Happens on Your 2nd Sweldo

If you're paid semi-monthly, your second sweldo resets the cycle. Enter the new amount, confirm your bills (or adjust if anything changed), and you get a fresh daily number.

Over time, you'll start to notice patterns: maybe the first half of the month is tighter because rent hits early, or the second half is looser because fewer bills are due.

## Your First 24 Hours

Here's what to do after setup:

1. **Log every expense today.** Coffee, jeep fare, lunch — everything. It takes 3 seconds each.
2. **Check your remaining allowance tonight.** See how the day went.
3. **Wake up tomorrow and check your new number.** If you underspent, it goes up. If you overspent, it adjusts down. No drama, just math.

By day three, you'll have a feel for your rhythm. By week two, you'll wonder how you ever budgeted any other way.
